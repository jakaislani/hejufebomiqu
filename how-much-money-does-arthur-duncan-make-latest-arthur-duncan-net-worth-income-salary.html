Arthur Duncan is a tap dancer most recognised for his role as the first African-American regular on a variety television show, The Lawrence Welk Show, which he held from 1964 to 1982. Duncan’s first foray into show business occurred when he was thirteen years old. He was a member of a dancing quartet that performed at Pasadena’s McKinley Junior High School. Duncan made a cameo appearance in the 1989 film Tap among other great tap dancers. Duncan was honoured at New York City’s annual “Tap Extravaganza” in 2004.
